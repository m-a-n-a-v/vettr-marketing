# What Is a VETTR Score?

Every stock on VETTR receives a **VETTR Score** — a single number between 0 and 100 that represents the overall health and investability of a mining or resource company. Think of it as a report card for the stock: the higher the score, the stronger the company looks across the metrics that matter most to diligent investors.

The VETTR Score is not a price prediction. It is a diagnostic tool that tells you whether a company's fundamentals, management quality, and filing history deserve your attention — or your caution.

## How Scores Are Calculated

The VETTR Score V2 is built from four weighted pillars, each analysing a different dimension of the company:

- **Financial Survival (35%)** — Cash runway using the FCF Rule (if Free Cash Flow > 0, the company receives a perfect cash runway score) and solvency measured by debt-to-assets ratio. Companies with strong free cash flow and low leverage score highest.
- **Operational Efficiency (25%)** — Sector-specific operational ratios tailored to each company type. Mining companies are evaluated on exploration-to-opex ratio, tech companies on R&D-to-opex ratio, and producers on gross profit margin. Separate scoring targets apply for pre-revenue companies (70% weight) and producers (30% weight).
- **Shareholder Structure (25%)** — Executive pedigree (30%), dilution penalty (30%), SEDI insider conviction with a dynamic adjuster (20%), and warrant overhang proximity (20%). This pillar captures whether insiders are aligned with shareholders or working against them.
- **Market Sentiment & Price Action (15%)** — Liquidity health (20%), technical momentum versus the 20-day moving average (25%), news velocity (15%), short squeeze risk (20%), and analyst consensus distance from current price (20%).

Each pillar generates a sub-score, and the weighted combination produces the final VETTR Score. When a pillar lacks sufficient data, a **Re-Weighting Safety Valve** redistributes that pillar's weight proportionally across the remaining pillars so that no score is unfairly penalised for missing information.

## What the Ranges Mean

| Score Range | Label | Color | What It Means |
|---|---|---|---|
| **90 – 100** | Exceptional | Dark Green (#198754) | Exceptional fundamentals, strong FCF/runway, insider buying, and bullish momentum. This company passes every pillar with high marks. |
| **75 – 89** | Healthy | Lime Green (#84CC16) | Healthy company with positive cash runway, moderate uptrend, and favorable analyst targets. Minor areas to watch but overall strong. |
| **50 – 74** | Neutral | Yellow (#FBBF24) | Awaiting catalyst, mixed technicals, or near warrant overhang ceiling. The company may need a trigger to move decisively. |
| **30 – 49** | High Risk | Orange (#F97316) | Short cash runway, heavy dilution, rising short interest, or breaking key moving averages. Multiple concerns warrant extra caution. |
| **0 – 29** | Toxic | Deep Red (#DC2626) | Imminent dilution threat, high short interest, insider dumping, and active downward momentum. Significant risk factors across multiple pillars. |

## How Often Do Scores Update?

VETTR Scores are recalculated whenever new data arrives — a fresh filing, an insider transaction report, or an updated financial statement. In practice, most scores update at least weekly, and you will receive an alert any time a stock you follow experiences a meaningful score change.

## Using VETTR Scores Effectively

The VETTR Score is designed to be a starting point, not the final word. Use it to quickly screen a watchlist, identify which companies deserve deeper research, and spot deterioration in stocks you already own. Combined with VETTR's Red Flag details and Executive Pedigree profiles, the score gives you a comprehensive due diligence framework — all from a single app.
